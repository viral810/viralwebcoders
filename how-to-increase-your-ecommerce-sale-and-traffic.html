How To Increase Your Ecommerce Sale And Traffic
07 Apr

Traffic on landing pages: Let the paid traffic be sent to the landing page for product , not to the homepage. Or make it go to the categories that match the user's’ needs.

SEO : Use product descriptions, and make sure they are both intriguing and inceptive. There are reasons for both of these rules.

The text description gives search engines something to index and well-picked keywords ensure that you are easily found. Creating unique descriptions prevents you from being accidentally filtered out as spam by search engines.

On-site Search: Attracting customers to your site is only half the crusade. Helping them find products on your page is the next step to close the sale. And the most effective way to do this is with an easy-to-find and easy-to-use search function. Utilize every aspect of design to help it stand out: placement, color, cursor focus, and the all-important white space. And make sure to do your own on-site keyword analysis to improve descriptions.

Enhance the loading time: The ideal loading time users prefer is less than 3 seconds. To achieve this, here are some points to keep in mind

1. Image optimization – Images over 30K in size destroys loading times, especially if they are abundant. Attempt to get the size below 30K without affecting the image quality.

2. Expiry for cache –Caching can make loading times slower. Try to set the expiry to utilize the local network opposed to the main network.

3. Remove the “dead” script – Be sure to remove all the useless coding. It will enhance the loading time. This is a standard clean-up task.

4. Remove duplicate scripts – Analyze the script to mitigate the chance of webpage accidently producing the duplicate scripts. This is very essential for loading time of the webpage.

No dead-ends: Not a single page on the website should be the absolute full stop. Every page should channel the visitors toward the right product, service or sales representative.
